EXPLORE THE WILDERNESS

Discover the incredible biodiversity thriving across Amuay Wildlife Park's 72 square kilometers of lush grasslands, hills and marshes. Encounter exotic birds, fascinating animals, unique insects, and rare plant species, all protected and flourishing in their natural home.

UNDER THE SPOTLIGHT

Scarlet Macaw

A large yellow, red and blue Neotropical parrot. It is a popular bird in aviculture as a result of its striking plumage

Capybara

The largest living rodent, native to South America

Araguaney

Venezuela’s national tree, famous for its spectacular blooming of vibrant golden-yellow flowers, usually marking the onset of the rainy season

Howler Monkey

Howler monkeys are New World monkeys known for their loud howls, which can be heard for miles and help maintain territory boundaries

Giant Otter

a South American carnivorous mammal and an endangered predator reaching up to 1.8 m

AMUAY WILDLIFE CONSERVATION

CONSERVATION IN ACTION!

Amuay Wildlife Park places conservation at the core of its mission, actively engaging in numerous efforts to preserve its unique biodiversity. Our dedicated team of park rangers and wildlife experts work tirelessly to monitor wildlife populations, restore habitats, and conduct ecological research to support ongoing conservation initiatives.

Come and explore, learn, and connect deeply with nature at Amuay Wildlife Park, where every visit contributes to the preservation of our planet's precious biodiversity.
