DISCOVER THE WILDERNESS

Discover an incredible biodiversity of animals and plants at Amuay Wildlife Park's 72 square kilometers of lush grasslands, hills and marshes. Encounter exotic birds, fascinating animals and rare endangered species, all protected and flourishing in their natural home.

Animals

Giant Anteater

One of four living species of anteaters. The giant anteater is 182 to 217 cm in length

Giant Anteater

Spectacled Bear

Also known as the South American Bear. It is the only living species of bear native to South America, and the last remaining short-faced bear

Spectacled Bear

Giant Otter

A South American carnivorous mammal and an endangered predator reaching up to 1.8 m

Giant Otter

Howler Monkey

Howler monkeys are New World monkeys known for their loud howls, which can be heard for miles and help maintain territory boundaries

Howler Monkey

Birds

Scarlet Macaw

A large yellow, red and blue Neotropical parrot. It is a popular bird in aviculture as a result of its striking plumage

Scarlet Macaw

Scarlet Ibis

A​​ species of ibis in the bird family Threskiornithidae. The striking red bird gets its colour from carotene found in the crustaceans on which it feeds

Scarlet Ibis

Keel-billed Toucan

Also know as sulfur-breasted toucan, is an omnivorous forest bird that feeds on fruits, seeds, insects

Keel-billed Toucan

New World Vulture

Scavenging birds found predominantly in the Americas, known for their featherless heads and keen sense of smell

New World Vulture

Plants & Trees

Celiba

A massive tree with a wide trunk, significant in indigenous mythology and ecosystems

Celiba

Camoruco

A large, deciduous tree found in forests, attracting macaws and other birds

Camoruco

Araguaney

Venezuela’s national tree, famous for its spectacular blooming of vibrant golden-yellow flowers, usually marking the onset of the rainy season

Araguaney

Carob Tree

Thrives in dry areas, providing food for birds and mammals

Carob Tree
